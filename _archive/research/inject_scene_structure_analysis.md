## Inject Structure Formula

### Standard Scene Format

```yaml
# Turn X: [Descriptive Title]
# [Date/Time] ([Time elapsed since start])
# [Source note or context]

id: turn_XXX_identifier
title: "Short Title"
description: |
  === [SCENE TYPE] ===
  [Date/Time repeated]
  
  [Opening Hook - Immediate Situation]
  
  [Factual Details - What happened]
  
  **[Specialist Assessment 1]:** [Intelligence/Military perspective]
  
  **[Specialist Assessment 2]:** [Technical/Operational details]
  
  **[Political Impact 1]:** [Home Secretary - Domestic]
  
  **[Political Impact 2]:** [Foreign Secretary - Diplomatic]
  
  **[Legal Framework]:** [Attorney General - Law]
  
  **[Strategic Synthesis]:** [NSA - Big picture]
  
  [Decision Prompt - Direct question to player]

channel: [briefing|intelligence|emergency|diplomatic|military]
effects:
  - metric: [metric_name]
    delta: [min..max or fixed value]
```

### Scene Type Headers by Urgency

- `=== CABINET OFFICE BRIEFING ROOM A (COBRA) ===` - Initial formal briefing
- `=== INTELLIGENCE UPDATE ===` - New information, elevated concern
- `=== EMERGENCY BRIEFING ===` - Critical incident, immediate response needed
- `=== EMERGENCY ALERT - HIGHEST PRIORITY ===` - Flash alert, minutes to decide
- `=== FLASH MESSAGE FROM [SOURCE] ===` - Urgent military/intelligence alert

### Opening Hook Patterns

| Urgency Level | Opening Line Example |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Measured | "Prime Minister, thank you for convening this emergency meeting..." |
| Elevated | "Prime Minister, we have a developing situation." |
| High | "Prime Minister, we have a critical incident." |
| Critical | "Prime Minister, we have a FLASH message from [source]." |
| Catastrophic | "Prime Minister, **[ALL CAPS EVENT]**" |

### Advisor Speaking Order

1. **Specialists first** (GCHQ, CDS, Intelligence) - Facts and assessment
2. **Political impact** (Home Sec, Foreign Sec) - Implications
3. **Legal framework** (Attorney General) - Constraints
4. **Strategic synthesis** (NSA) - Big picture, decision framing

### Decision Prompt Patterns

- **Early turns**: "How do you wish to respond?"
- **Mid turns**: "Prime Minister, what are your orders?"
- **Late turns**: "We have [X] minutes. What do you decide?"
- **Critical turns**: "The next [X] minutes will determine whether we're at war."

## Narrative Techniques

### 1. Specificity Over Vagueness

**Good**: "At 02:57 this morning, 12 nautical miles off Orkney, a Kilo-class submarine..."  
**Bad**: "Recently, near Scotland, a submarine..."

### 2. Multiple Voices

Each advisor has distinct perspective:
- **CDS**: Military capability, force readiness, tactical options
- **NSA**: Intelligence assessment, strategic implications, threat analysis
- **Foreign Sec**: Diplomatic channels, alliance cohesion, international law
- **Home Sec**: Domestic security, public safety, civil protection
- **Attorney General**: Legal framework, international law, rules of engagement

### 3. Layered Information

1. **What happened** (facts)
2. **What it means** (assessment)
3. **What could happen** (implications)
4. **What we should do** (options)

### 4. Time Stamps and Updates

Use real-time updates within same inject:
```
**[UPDATED - 02:59]**

**CDS:** Prime Minister, updated trajectory analysis suggests...
```

### 5. Formatting for Urgency

- `**MISSILE LAUNCH DETECTED**` - All caps + bold for critical alerts
- Bullet points for immediate impact lists
- Short paragraphs when time pressure is high
- Longer exposition when deliberation is possible

### 6. Human Element

Always include civilian/human perspective:
- Witnesses (ferry passengers, social media)
- Public reaction (panic buying, sheltering, resilience)
- Casualties (specific numbers, not "many")
- Infrastructure impact (2M homes, hospitals on backup power)

## Russian Strategy Pattern

### Primary Objective
Neutralize UK as NATO obstacle **without triggering Article 5**

### Tactics
1. **Calibrated strikes** (not all-out war)
2. **Test NATO resolve incrementally**
3. **Exploit UK military weaknesses**
4. **Psychological warfare via media**
5. **Ultimatums with tight deadlines**
6. **Nuclear threats** (but not use)

### Escalation Ladder
1. Covert operations (assassinations, sabotage)
2. Infrastructure attacks (power, transport)
3. Military provocations (submarine surfacing)
4. Conventional missile strikes (military targets)
5. Civilian targets (Heathrow, Oxford Circus)
6. Nuclear ultimatum (threat only)

---

## UK Constraints (Exploited by Russia)

### Military
- Limited missile stocks (days, not weeks)
- Only 2 simultaneous air patrols for entire UK
- Type-45 destroyers: 6 total, typically only 2 operational
- Attack submarines: 5 total, only 2 deployable
- F-35s require tanker support for range
- No Iron Dome equivalent (£25bn+ to build)

### Political
- US commitment uncertain (Trump-era scenario)
- NATO divided (some support, some urge restraint)
- Public panic vs resilience ("Blitz spirit")
- Legal constraints (international law, proportionality)
- Nuclear deterrent: when to threaten vs use?

### Strategic
- No "war book" (national defence plan abandoned post-Cold War)
- Ammunition stockpiles depleted
- Civil defence infrastructure dismantled
- Emergency services unprepared for mass casualties
- Critical infrastructure vulnerable

## Application for Dynamic Inject Generation

### For LLM-Generated Injects (Post-Turn 5)

**The LLM should:**
1. **Follow the scene structure formula** (header → situation → assessments → prompt)
2. **Maintain escalation patterns** (each turn raises stakes)
3. **Adapt to player decisions** (if player invoked Article 4, Russia tests NATO resolve)
4. **Use specific details** (times, locations, numbers)
5. **Include civilian element** (human impact, not just military)
6. **Vary time pressure** (hours → minutes → seconds as crisis escalates)
7. **Reference conversation history** (callbacks to earlier decisions)

**The LLM should NOT:**
1. Generate generic/vague scenarios
2. Ignore player's previous actions
3. Break established constraints (UK capabilities, Russian strategy)
4. Skip advisor perspectives
5. Forget the human/civilian element

### Example Prompt Structure for LLM

```

- Test NATO resolve (Article 4 activated but not Article 5)
```

## Conclusion

The podcast follows a clear **escalation arc** over 21 hours:
- **Provocation** (Turns 1-5): Covert ops, infrastructure, missile threats
- **War** (Turns 6-7): Actual strikes, UK retaliation
- **Ultimatum** (Turns 8-9): Nuclear threats, 1-hour deadline
- **Collapse** (Turns 10-11): Second wave, ceasefire

Each inject uses a **consistent structure**:
- Scene header with urgency level
- Immediate situation hook
- Factual details (specific times, locations, numbers)
- Multiple advisor perspectives
- Direct decision prompt

The **civilian element** escalates from ominous signs to mass casualties, maintaining human stakes throughout.

**Time pressure** compresses from hours (early turns) to minutes (late turns), forcing rapid decisions under stress.

This structure should inform all future inject generation, whether pre-scripted or LLM-generated.
