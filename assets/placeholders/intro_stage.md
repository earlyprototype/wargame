# FALSE FLAG: THE WARGAME

===============================================================================
## SCENE I: SEVEROMORSK NAVAL BASE, RUSSIA
## 72 Hours Earlier — Thursday, 2nd October 2025, 03:15 Local Time

The Barents Sea lies black and restless beneath a thin crescent moon. At the mouth of 
Kola Bay, the silhouettes of seven ballistic missile submarines slip silently from their 
moorings, one after another, like great whales departing into the deep.

Their conning towers cut dark shapes against the receding cliffs of the Kola Peninsula. 
Choppy waves catch fragments of moonlight, breaking silver against the submarines' hulls 
before they submerge. Within minutes, only roiling wakes mark their passage.

On the pier, a lone figure watches through night-vision binoculars. Emerging from the 
shadows of the coastline, the entire Northern Fleet drives silently towards a sleeping 
United Kingdom. The largest submarine force Russia has sent to sea since the Cold War 
carries out its orders — the first, unalterable act of Operation Tuman.

Operation Tuman — "Fog" — has begun.

===============================================================================
## SCENE II: JOINT OPERATIONS CENTRE, NORTHWOOD
## Sunday, 5th October 2025, 16:45

Deep beneath Northwood Headquarters, the Joint Operations Centre hums with controlled 
urgency. Wall-sized displays show the North Atlantic in real-time: blue icons for Allied 
forces, red for Russian. The red icons have multiplied dramatically in the past 72 hours.

A Royal Navy commander briefs the duty officer: "Fifteen submarines, sir. Seven SSBNs, 
eight SSNs. Plus surface escorts. They're forming up southwest of Iceland, heading 
toward the GIUK gap."

"Fifteen?" The duty officer's voice is tight. "When did we last see numbers like this?"

"Never, sir. Not in peacetime. Not even during the Cold War."

The commander pauses, then adds quietly: "And that's just what we can track."

A secure phone rings. The duty officer answers, listens, then sets it down carefully.

"Get COBRA on the line. The Prime Minister needs to see this. Now."

===============================================================================
## SCENE III: CABINET OFFICE BRIEFING ROOM A (COBRA)
## Sunday, 5th October 2025, 17:00

You are the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.

You sit at the head of a long mahogany table in the secure briefing room beneath 
70 Whitehall. The room is windowless, lit by harsh fluorescents that cast everything 
in stark relief. A large digital display dominates the far wall: a map of the North 
Atlantic, scattered with red icons clustering ominously near British waters.

The air is thick with tension. No one speaks as you enter. They're waiting for you.

Around the table, your key advisors:

• [cyan bold]THE NATIONAL SECURITY ADVISOR[/cyan bold] sits to your right, leaning forward with hands clasped.
  A former diplomat with decades of experience, sharp eyes constantly assessing.

    - Coordinates intelligence and policy across government
    - Your strategic compass when the nation faces crisis


• [cyan bold]THE CHIEF OF THE DEFENCE STAFF[/cyan bold] sits across from you in service dress uniform.
  Calm, methodical, with operational experience spanning multiple conflicts.

    - Commands the UK Armed Forces
    - Military judgement critical if escalation occurs


• [cyan bold]THE HOME SECRETARY[/cyan bold] sits to your left, tablet open, messages streaming in.

    - Responsible for domestic security and counter-terrorism
    - If Russian agents operate on UK soil, this is their domain


• [cyan bold]THE FOREIGN SECRETARY[/cyan bold] sits beside them, diplomatic cables spread across the table.

    - Manages relations with allies and adversaries
    - May need to call in every favour and treaty obligation


• [cyan bold]THE ATTORNEY GENERAL[/cyan bold] sits at the far end, legal pad covered in notes.

    - Advises on the legal framework for action
    - Whatever you decide must be defensible under international law
    - Your conscience in matters of war and peace


Several junior officials line the walls, ready with briefing materials. The Cabinet 
Secretary stands near the door, guardian of proper process even in crisis.

The National Security Advisor clears their throat. The room falls silent.

"Prime Minister, thank you for convening this emergency session on short notice."

They gesture to the display. The red icons seem to pulse with menace.

"We have a situation."

## YOUR ROLE

You are the Prime Minister. The decisions you make in the coming hours will determine 
whether the United Kingdom faces down this threat, stumbles into war, or worse — appears 
weak before an adversary testing our resolve.

Your advisors will provide counsel. Intelligence will flow in. Events will unfold whether 
you act or not. But the decisions — and their consequences — are yours alone.

You must balance competing imperatives:

  • Defend the realm without provoking escalation
  • Maintain alliance cohesion while NATO's commitment wavers  
  • Project strength while managing limited military capabilities
  • Keep the public informed without causing panic
  • Act within the law while facing an adversary who does not

The weight of the nation rests on your shoulders. Your advisors are waiting.

The crisis has begun.
